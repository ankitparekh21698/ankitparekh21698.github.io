Publication

May 14, 2020 - IEEE Xplore - 3rd International Conference on Information and Computer Technologies (ICICT) 2020 held at San Francisco, USA.

Explainable Deep-Fake Detection Using Visual Interpretability Methods

In this paper, a framework to detect Deep-Fake videos using a Deep Learning Approach is proposed by training a Convolutional Neural Network architecture on a database of extracted faces from FaceForensics' DeepFakeDetection Dataset. We tested the model on various Explainable AI techniques such as LRP and LIME to provide crisp visualizations of the salient regions through heatmaps and image slices of the image focused on by the model. We implemented a pipeline to classify faces of actors in videos and demonstrated an approach to building a model that is robust to noise and rotationally invariant. The prospective and elusive goal is to localize the facial manipulations caused by Faceswaps. We hope to use this approach to build trust between AI and Human agents and to demonstrate the applicability of XAI in various real-life scenarios.
